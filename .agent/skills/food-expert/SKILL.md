# SKILL: Local Food Business Architect

## 1. Skill Meta
**Name:** Local Food Business Architect (The "Culinarian" Engine)
**Description:** Extreme proficiency in architecting digital storefronts for the hospitality industry. This skill prioritizes the "First Bite" — ensuring the user feels the texture, smell, and atmosphere of the physical shop through the screen.

## 2. The Culinarian Protocol
*   **The "First Bite" Hero**: Use extreme close-ups of food textures (macro photography) or high-quality video of steam/motion.
*   **Menu Over-Engineering**: Menus must not be static PDFs. They must be interactive, searchable, and use high-end typography (Categorized, e.g., "Mains", "Sides", "Libations").
*   **Contextual Conversion**: Every food item should have a "Buy Now" or "Pre-Order" micro-interaction. Use "Goldbelly-style" buttons for national shipping.
*   **The "Vibe" Section**: Include 1:1 atmospheric photography of the physical space (interior lighting, sourdough rising, coffee pouring).

## 3. Engineering Guidelines

### A. Local SEO Integration
*   **Schema.org/Restaurant**: Mandatory JSON-LD for opening hours, price range, and address.
*   **Maps Injection**: A "Floating Map Island" that shows the shop's physical location with a 1-click "Directions" trigger.

### B. Reservation Architecture
*   **The "Table-One" Flow**: A floating, pill-shaped reservation widget that anchors to the bottom-right.
*   **Wait-List Typewriter**: A live monospace ticker showing current wait times (e.g. `[STATUS: 20 MIN WAIT]`).

### C. Menu Patterns
*   **Digital Chalkboard**: A high-density, monospace font section for "Daily Specials" that feels reactive and fresh.
*   **Ingredients Narrative**: Hovering over an item reveals its source (e.g., "Flour from Mill City, MN").

## 4. Visual Constants
*   **Warmth over Cold**: Avoid blue colors unless it's a seafood brand. Use "Baked-Goods" browns, "Fresh-Sage" greens, and "Wine-Spill" reds.
*   **Typeface**: Pair a "Rust-style" serif (Fraunces) with a tactical monospace (JetBrains Mono).
